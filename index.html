Dartmouth Orca Whale Enthusiasts

Welcome to the homepage of the Dartmouth Orca Whale Enthusiasts (DOWE), Dartmouth's premier club for enjoying and discussing all things Orca related. The DOWE provides a positive environment to explore your interest in Orca Whales, hosting weekly meetings to discuss these beautiful creatures. The DOWE also embarks on termly whale watching expeditions in order to experience the nature up close and personal. This website is currently under construction, however please feel free to enjoy the following images of Orcas as well as some interesting related facts.

The orca, or "killer whale" (Orcinus orca) is a toothed whale and is the largest member of the dolphin family. It is highly social and composed of matrilineal family groups.

Orcas feed on fish, squid, birds, and marine mammals. Like dolphins, orcas use echolocation - bouncing sound off of objects to determine their location - to hunt and use a series of high-pitched clicks to stun prey.

Like humans, Orcas ocassionally need to let loose to relieve the stress of a busy work week. As a result of this, many underwater Orca nightclubs have sprung up over recent years.
